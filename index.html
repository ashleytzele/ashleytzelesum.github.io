About Me.

I’m a sophomore Computer Science student at San Francisco State University with a strong interest in building practical and forward-looking software. I enjoy working on projects that combine AI with real-world applications, particularly in health and sports.

My background as a national-level swimmer has shaped how I approach problem-solving — staying composed under pressure while focusing on performance, efficiency, and continuous improvement. I’m especially interested in how modern training philosophies in sports are evolving toward a more data-driven approach, where technology and analytics play a key role in improving performance. This perspective influences how I design and build systems today — prioritizing quality, precision, and meaningful results over quantity.

Beyond swimming, I’m passionate about sports like basketball and baseball, where the growing role of statistics and data is transforming how the game is understood. I’m also a stan Formula 1 superfan, especially backing the team in red — drawn to the speed, adrenaline, and strategic precision that define the sport.
